# GEOinsightLab
Code Repository of the PhD Thesis

This repository contains the notebooks developed as part of the **GEOInsightLab** project, which forms an integral part of the PhD thesis entitled *"Contributions of Data Science to a (New) Approach to Space"*, by Roberto Miguel de Oliveira Machado. This research was conducted under the **PhD Program in Geography and Territorial Planning** at **Universidade Nova de Lisboa**, with a specialization in **Remote Sensing and Geographic Information Systems**.

All notebooks follow a **sequential process**, aligned with the methodological and technological framework described in Chapter III of the thesis.

---

## 📁 Notebooks Structure

### `Initial_setup.ipynb`
> Sets up the analysis environment: libraries, paths, utility functions, and initial configurations.

---

### `data_exploration.ipynb`
> Final version of the exploratory analysis pipeline for geographic data.

### `processo/data_exploration_base.ipynb`
> Full development of the exploratory analysis, including variable preparation and data integration.

---

### `clustering.ipynb`
> Building clusters of residential buildings based on geographic and sociodemographic variables.

### `processo/clustering_base.ipynb`
> Detailed development of clustering methods: K-Means, GMM, Agglomerative, and evaluation criteria.

---

### `explainable_ai_clustering.ipynb`
> Application of Explainable AI (XAI) techniques to clustering: LIME, SHAP, and Decision Trees.

### `processo/explainable_ai_clustering_base.ipynb`
> Full implementation and advanced tests using different interpretable algorithms and complementary analyses.

---

### `supervised_modeling.ipynb`
> Predictive modeling using supervised algorithms such as XGBoost, focused on territorial attractiveness.

### `processo/supervised_modeling_base.ipynb`
> Detailed development of supervised modeling, including model validation and hyperparameter tuning.

---

### `xgb_agent_distribution_explained.ipynb`
> Analysis of agent distribution using the XGBoost model and interpretation with SHAP values.

### `processo/xgb_agent_distribution_explained_base.ipynb`
> In-depth implementation of explainable analysis on the territorial attractiveness model.

---

### `insights.ipynb`
> Final consolidation of spatial insights generated by the platform based on data and modeling.

### `processo/insights_base.ipynb`
> Complete development of analytical synthesis and spatial interpretation.

---

### `answers_and_usability.ipynb`
> Results from the usability and learning assessment of participants in the experimental workshop.

### `processo/answers_and_usability_base.ipynb`
> Detailed code and analysis of evaluation forms, surveys, and performance metrics.

---

### `processo/case_study_prep.ipynb`
> Notebook used to prepare the case study – preliminary tests and data setup for the municipality of Porto.

---

## 📂 Data Sources

All `.csv` files used in the notebooks were obtained through spatial pre-processing procedures available in the following GitHub repositories:

- [Residential Buildings](https://github.com/RobertoOlivetree/Residential_Buildings.git)
- [Average Distance to Services by Category](https://github.com/RobertoOlivetree/Average_Distance_to_Services_by_Category.git)
- [Population Distribution](https://github.com/RobertoOlivetree/Population_distribution.git)

These datasets were integrated and processed as documented in Chapters III and IV of the PhD thesis.
